## Description
Academic paper (arXiv:2601.20245) exploring how AI assistance produces significant productivity gains across professional domains, particularly for novice workers. Investigates how this assistance affects the development of skills required to effectively supervise AI systems.

## Key Findings
- AI assistance produces significant productivity gains, especially for novice workers
- The effect on skill formation required to supervise AI effectively remains unclear
- Comprehension testing shows degradation when AI is used for passive code generation vs. active conceptual inquiry
- Developers using AI for **code generation delegation** score below 40% on comprehension tests
- Developers using AI for **conceptual inquiry** score above 65%
