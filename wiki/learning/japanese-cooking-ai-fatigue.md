## Summary
Takuya's personal essay connecting the Japanese cooking principle of *kanso* (simplicity/elimination of the non-essential) to developer wellbeing in the AI era. Argues that sustainable creativity requires intentional boundaries with AI tools.

## Core Concept: Kanso
*Kanso* (簡素) is a Japanese aesthetic principle meaning simplicity or elimination of the non-essential. Applied to development:
- Not every task needs AI assistance
- Some work is worth doing manually to preserve craft and understanding
- Boundaries prevent burnout from relentless automation

## Key Takeaways
- AI fatigue is real; relentless automation can erode creative satisfaction.
- Applying minimalist principles helps developers decide when to delegate and when to craft manually.
- Mental health and productivity are not mutually exclusive.
- Intentional boundaries with AI tools preserve both wellbeing and skill.

## Recommended Audience
Developers feeling burnout from constant AI-assisted workflows and content creators balancing automation with craft.
