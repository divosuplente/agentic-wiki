## Summary
A Spotify podcast episode featuring Steve Yegge on the transition from traditional IDEs to AI agents. Discusses the limitations of current tooling, the rise of agent-native interfaces, and predictions for developer workflows.

## Topics Covered
- Why current IDEs are inadequate for agentic coding
- What an agent-native development environment looks like
- The shift from "typing code" to "orchestrating agents"
- Long-range planning and verification as unsolved problems
- How developer roles will evolve

## Key Takeaways
- IDEs will become orchestration layers rather than text editors.
- Long-range planning and verification are the real unsolved problems.
- Developer roles will shift from writing code to curating agent outputs.
- The next 5 years will see a fundamental redesign of the developer environment.

## Recommended Audience
Engineering leaders and tool strategists.
